# D&D 5E BASIC RULES (2014)

This document contains the core rules of Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition (2014) for use by an AI Dungeon Master (DM).

## THE CORE RULE
1. Roll a d20.
2. Add any relevant modifiers (Ability Modifier, Proficiency Bonus).
3. Compare the total to a target number (Difficulty Class/DC or Armor Class/AC).
If the total equals or exceeds the target, the roll is a success.

---

## CHAPTER 1: STEP-BY-STEP CHARACTERS

### 1. Choose a Race
- **Racial Traits**: Natural talents, special senses (like Darkvision), and proficiencies.
- **Ability Score Increase**: Most races increase one or more ability scores.
- **Languages & Speed**: Note your starting languages and base walking speed.

### 2. Choose a Class
- **Class Features**: Abilities that set your class apart (e.g., Spellcasting, Sneak Attack).
- **Proficiencies**: Armor, weapons, skills, and saving throws gained from your class.
- **Hit Points**: Determined by your class's Hit Die and your Constitution modifier.

### 3. Determine Ability Scores
- **Generation**: Roll 4d6 and record the total of the highest three. Do this 6 times.
- **Standard Array**: Alternatively, use 15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8.
- **Modifiers**: (Ability Score - 10) / 2, rounded down.

### 4. Describe Your Character
- **Details**: Name, physical appearance, and personality.
- **Alignment**: Moral and ethical outlook (e.g., Lawful Good, Chaotic Neutral).
- **Background**: Your character’s origin. Grants a feature and proficiency in two skills.
- **Personal Characteristics**: Ideals (beliefs), Bonds (connections), and Flaws (weaknesses).

### 5. Choose Equipment
- **Starting Gear**: Provided by your class and background.
- **Carrying Capacity**: Your Strength score multiplied by 15 is the weight (in pounds) you can carry.

### 6. Come Together
- Heroic characters work together in a **Party**.

## CHAPTER 7: USING ABILITY SCORES

### Six Abilities
- **Strength**: Physical power, athletic training. (Skills: Athletics)
- **Dexterity**: Agility, reflexes, balance. (Skills: Acrobatics, Sleight of Hand, Stealth)
- **Constitution**: Endurance, health, vital force. (No skills)
- **Intelligence**: Reasoning, memory, logic. (Skills: Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion)
- **Wisdom**: Perception, insight, intuition. (Skills: Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Perception, Survival)
- **Charisma**: Force of personality, persuasion. (Skills: Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion)

### Ability Modifiers
Score | Modifier
--- | ---
1 | -5
2–3 | -4
4–5 | -3
6–7 | -2
8–9 | -1
10–11 | +0
12–13 | +1
14–15 | +2
16–17 | +3
18–19 | +4
20 | +5

### Advantage and Disadvantage
- **Advantage**: Roll two d20s and use the higher result.
- **Disadvantage**: Roll two d20s and use the lower result.
- If both apply, they cancel out (roll normally). Multiple instances of advantage or disadvantage do not stack.

### Proficiency Bonus
Added to:
- Attack rolls with weapons you are proficient with.
- Ability checks with skills or tools you are proficient with.
- Saving throws you are proficient with.
- Saving throw DCs for spells you cast.

## CHAPTER 8: ADVENTURING

### Time
- **Round**: 6 seconds (used in combat).
- **Short Rest**: At least 1 hour. Allows spending Hit Dice to heal.
- **Long Rest**: At least 8 hours. Restores all HP and half of max Hit Dice.

### Movement
- **Speed**: The distance (in feet) a creature can move in one round.
- **Difficult Terrain**: Every foot of movement costs 1 extra foot.

### Social Interaction
- NPC attitudes can be **Friendly**, **Indifferent**, or **Hostile**.
- Use Charisma checks (Persuasion, Deception, Intimidation) to influence NPCs.

---

## CHAPTER 9: COMBAT

### Order of Combat
1. **Determine Surprise**: Stealth checks vs. Passive Perception.
2. **Establish Positions**.
3. **Roll Initiative**: Dexterity check.
4. **Take Turns**: In initiative order.
5. **Begin Next Round**.

### Actions in Combat
- **Attack**: Melee or Ranged.
- **Cast a Spell**: Casting time determines if it's an action, bonus action, or reaction.
- **Dash**: Double your movement speed for the turn.
- **Disengage**: Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks.
- **Dodge**: Attacks against you have disadvantage; DEX saves have advantage.
- **Help**: Give an ally advantage on their next check or attack.
- **Hide**: Dexterity (Stealth) check.
- **Ready**: Prepare an action to be triggered by a specific event.
- **Search**: Wisdom (Perception) or Intelligence (Investigation) check.
- **Use an Object**: Interact with a second object (first is free).

### Damage and Healing
- **Attack Roll**: 1d20 + Ability Mod + Proficiency Bonus vs. Target AC.
- **Critical Hit**: Roll a 20 on the d20. Roll all of the attack’s damage dice twice.
- **Death Saving Throws**: At 0 HP, roll a d20 (no modifiers). 10+ is a success. 3 successes = stable; 3 failures = dead. Rolling a 1 counts as 2 failures. Rolling a 20 restores 1 HP.

---

## CHAPTER 10: SPELLCASTING

### Spell Slots
- Casting a spell expends a slot of the spell's level or higher.
- Cantrips are 0-level spells that do not use slots.

### Components
- **V** (Verbal): Spoken words.
- **S** (Somatic): Hand gestures.
- **M** (Material): Physical objects or a focus.

### Duration
- **Instantaneous**: Effect happens and ends immediately.
- **Concentration**: Lasts until broken or duration ends. Taking damage requires a Constitution save (DC 10 or half damage taken) to maintain concentration.

## APPENDIX: CONDITIONS

- **Blinded**: Cannot see. Fails sight checks. Attackers have advantage; its attacks have disadvantage.
- **Charmed**: Cannot attack the charmer. Charmer has advantage on social checks.
- **Deafened**: Cannot hear. Fails hearing checks.
- **Frightened**: Disadvantage on checks/attacks while source is in sight. Cannot move closer to source.
- **Grappled**: Speed becomes 0. Ends if grappler is incapacitated.
- **Incapacitated**: Cannot take actions or reactions.
- **Invisible**: Impossible to see. Heavily obscured. Attackers have disadvantage; its attacks have advantage.
- **Paralyzed**: Incapacitated. Cannot move/speak. Fails STR/DEX saves. Attacks against it have advantage and are crits if within 5ft.
- **Petrified**: Incapacitated. Transformed to stone. Fails STR/DEX saves. Resistance to all damage. Immune to poison/disease.
- **Poisoned**: Disadvantage on attacks and ability checks.
- **Prone**: Movement is crawling. Disadvantage on attacks. Attackers within 5ft have advantage; others have disadvantage.
- **Restrained**: Speed 0. Attackers have advantage; its attacks have disadvantage. Disadvantage on DEX saves.
- **Stunned**: Incapacitated. Cannot move. Speaks falteringly. Fails STR/DEX saves. Attackers have advantage.
- **Unconscious**: Incapacitated. Drops items. Prone. Fails STR/DEX saves. Attackers have advantage and crits if within 5ft.
